# ISO 9001:2015 — Compliance Reference

> **Standard:** ISO 9001:2015 — Quality Management Systems (QMS)
> **Scope:** Requirements for a quality management system where an organization needs to demonstrate its ability to consistently provide products and services that meet customer and applicable statutory and regulatory requirements
> **Official source:** [ISO 9001:2015](https://www.iso.org/standard/62085.html)

## 1. What ISO 9001 Requires

ISO 9001:2015 is the most widely adopted management system standard globally, with over 1 million certificates issued. It is structured around the **Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA)** cycle and is built on **seven quality management principles**:

1. **Customer focus** — Meeting and exceeding customer expectations
2. **Leadership** — Establishing unity of purpose and direction
3. **Engagement of people** — Competent, empowered, engaged people at all levels
4. **Process approach** — Managing activities as interrelated processes
5. **Improvement** — Ongoing focus on improvement
6. **Evidence-based decision making** — Decisions based on analysis of data and information
7. **Relationship management** — Managing relationships with interested parties

**Clauses with requirements (4–10):**

| Clause | Title | Key Requirements |
|--------|-------|-----------------|
| 4 | Context of the organization | Understanding the organization, interested parties, QMS scope, process approach |
| 5 | Leadership | Management commitment, quality policy, organizational roles and responsibilities |
| 6 | Planning | Actions to address risks and opportunities, quality objectives, planning of changes |
| 7 | Support | Resources, competence, awareness, communication, documented information |
| 8 | Operation | Operational planning, requirements for products/services, design and development, control of externally provided processes, production and service provision, release, control of nonconforming outputs |
| 9 | Performance evaluation | Monitoring, measurement, analysis, evaluation; customer satisfaction; internal audit; management review |
| 10 | Improvement | General, nonconformity and corrective action, continual improvement |

## 3. Where Observability Provides Evidence

ISO 9001 auditors require **objective evidence** that the QMS is operating effectively — not just documented procedures, but proof of consistent execution. The [observability platform](../otel-contract.md) provides this evidence continuously:

| ISO 9001 Evidence Need | Observability Source | Clause Reference |
|------------------------|---------------------|------------------|
| Process performance monitoring | OTel traces across all 4 process loops with latency, throughput, and error metrics | 9.1.1 |
| Quality objective measurement | SLO dashboards, error budget burn rates, mission outcome metrics | 6.2, 9.1.1 |
| Traceability of products/services | End-to-end traces from mission intake through delivery with `mission.id` correlation | 8.5.2 |
| Control of changes | Git PR history + CI/CD pipeline traces showing governed change process | 6.3, 8.5.6 |
| Release verification | Quality gate pass/fail results in deployment spans, progressive rollout health checks | 8.6 |
| Nonconforming output detection | Automated anomaly detection, quality evaluation failures, incident alerts | 8.7 |
| Corrective action effectiveness | Before/after metrics comparison via dashboards; regression detection alerts | 10.2 |
| Management review inputs | Aggregated dashboards covering process performance, conformity, customer feedback, risk status, audit results | 9.3.2 |
| Internal audit evidence | Quality evaluation results, agent behavioral eval scores, policy compliance metrics | 9.2 |
| Competence verification | Agent performance metrics per type, task success rates, escalation patterns | 7.2 |
| Communication effectiveness | Signal processing latency, cross-layer handoff metrics, decision cycle times | 7.4 |
| Document control | Git version history with immutable audit trail, version bump CI enforcement | 7.5 |

## 4. Adopter Responsibilities

| Requirement | ISO 9001 Requirement | What's Needed | Criticality |
|-----|---------------------|---------------|-------------|
| **Formal QMS scope statement** | Clause 4.3 — must define boundaries and applicability of the QMS, including exclusions with justification | Dedicated QMS scope document that maps organizational boundaries to QMS applicability; must address which processes, products, services, locations, and functions are covered | High |
| **Customer satisfaction measurement** | Clause 9.1.2 — must monitor customer perceptions of the degree to which their needs and expectations have been fulfilled | Structured customer satisfaction measurement mechanism (surveys, feedback loops, NPS); the framework is primarily internal-facing and lacks a formal external customer feedback pipeline | Medium |
| **Management review with specific inputs per 9.3.2** | Clause 9.3.2 — management review inputs must include: status of actions from previous reviews, changes in external/internal issues, quality performance data, adequacy of resources, effectiveness of risk actions, improvement opportunities | Formalized management review record template that ensures all required inputs from 9.3.2 a) through g) are systematically collected and reviewed; current steering cycle covers many of these but lacks the structured record format | Medium |
| **External provider evaluation criteria** | Clause 8.4.1 — must establish criteria for evaluation, selection, monitoring of performance, and re-evaluation of external providers | Extend vendor risk management beyond security/risk focus to include quality performance criteria (delivery quality, defect rates, conformity of provided processes); current policy focuses on risk tiers, not quality output assessment | Medium |
| **Documented quality objectives at relevant functions and levels** | Clause 6.2.1 — quality objectives must be established at relevant functions, levels, and processes; must be measurable, monitored, communicated, and updated | Explicit quality objectives register that maps objectives to organizational levels (layers, divisions) with measurement methods, responsible parties, and timelines; currently implicit in mission briefs but not systematically tracked | Medium |
| **Formal internal audit programme** | Clause 9.2.2 — must plan, establish, implement, and maintain an audit programme including frequency, methods, responsibilities, and reporting | Scheduled internal audit programme with defined frequency, scope rotation, auditor independence criteria, and audit report template; quality evaluations serve a similar purpose but lack the structured programme wrapper | Medium |
| **Calibration of monitoring and measuring resources** | Clause 7.1.5 — measuring equipment must be calibrated or verified at specified intervals | Not directly applicable to software-based operations; document applicability determination with justification for exclusion in QMS scope statement | Low |
| **Organizational knowledge management** | Clause 7.1.6 — must determine the knowledge necessary for the operation of its processes and achievement of conformity | While the framework version-controls all knowledge, there is no explicit process for identifying, maintaining, and making available required organizational knowledge as a managed resource | Low |

**Addressed by framework:** Formal QMS scope statement guidance ([guide](guides/iso-9001-qms-scope.md)).
