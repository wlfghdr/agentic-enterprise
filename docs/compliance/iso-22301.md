# ISO 22301:2019 — Compliance Reference

> **Standard:** ISO 22301:2019 — Business Continuity Management Systems (BCMS)
> **Scope:** Requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving a business continuity management system
> **Official source:** [ISO 22301:2019](https://www.iso.org/standard/75106.html)

## 1. What ISO 22301 Requires

ISO 22301 follows the Annex SL high-level management system structure (shared with ISO 27001, ISO 42001, ISO 9001, etc.) with business continuity-specific extensions:

**Management System (Clauses 4–10):**
- Context of the organization, scope of the BCMS, interested parties (4)
- Leadership commitment, BC policy, organizational roles and responsibilities (5)
- Actions to address risks and opportunities, BC objectives, planning of changes (6)
- Support — resources, competence, awareness, communication, documented information (7)
- Operation — Business Impact Analysis (BIA), risk assessment, BC strategies and solutions, BC plans and procedures, exercise and testing programme (8)
- Performance evaluation — monitoring, measurement, internal audit, management review (9)
- Improvement — nonconformity, corrective action, continual improvement (10)

**Clause 8 is the heart of ISO 22301** — it goes beyond generic management system requirements to prescribe specific BC activities:
- **8.2 — Business Impact Analysis and risk assessment:** Identify critical activities, assess impacts of disruption, set prioritized timeframes for resumption (MTPD, RTO, RPO)
- **8.3 — BC strategies and solutions:** Determine strategies to protect, stabilize, continue, resume, and recover prioritized activities
- **8.4 — BC plans and procedures:** Document plans covering response structure, activation criteria, communication, resource requirements, and recovery procedures
- **8.5 — Exercise programme:** Validate BC plans through exercises consistent with BC objectives, with documented results and post-exercise reviews

### Clause-Level Mapping

| 6.3 | Planning of changes | Change management through PR-based governance, mission briefs for significant changes | Git PR history, mission artifacts |

## 3. Where Observability Provides Evidence

ISO 22301 auditors require evidence that BC arrangements are maintained, exercised, and effective — not just documented. The observability platform provides runtime evidence for several key requirements:

| Evidence Need | Observability Source | ISO 22301 Clause |
|---------------|---------------------|------------------|
| Service availability and uptime | SLO dashboards, availability metrics per tier | 8.2 (impact assessment), 9.1 (monitoring) |
| Recovery time measurement | DR drill spans with recovery timestamp deltas | 8.5 (exercise results), 8.2 (RTO validation) |
| Incident detection and response | Incident timeline OTel spans — detect/acknowledge/mitigate/resolve | 8.4 (BC plan activation), 9.1 (monitoring) |
| Escalation effectiveness | `governance.decision` span events with escalation reasoning | 8.4 (response structure) |
| Risk threshold breaches | KRI dashboards, `risk.threshold.breach` span events | 6.1 (risk monitoring), 9.1 (measurement) |
| Vendor health monitoring | Vendor SLA monitoring dashboards, dependency availability | 8.3 (supply chain strategies) |
| Change impact on availability | Deployment OTel spans, rollout health metrics, error budget consumption | 6.3 (planning of changes) |
| Communication effectiveness | Agent telemetry showing signal propagation, notification spans | 7.4 (communication) |
| Exercise evidence | DR drill execution spans with pass/fail outcomes and recovery time measurements | 8.5 (exercise programme) |
| Continuous improvement evidence | Signal→mission conversion rates, retrospective action item completion | 10.1–10.2 (improvement) |

## 4. Remaining Gaps

| Gap | ISO 22301 Requirement | What's Needed | Severity |
|-----|----------------------|---------------|----------|
| **Formal BCMS scope statement** | Clause 4.3 | Deployment must define BCMS boundaries — which products, services, activities, and locations are in scope | High — required for certification |
| **Business Impact Analysis template** | Clause 8.2.2 | Formal BIA methodology and template: identify critical activities, determine MTPD, set RTO/RPO per activity, identify dependencies and resources | High — central to ISO 22301 |
| **Documented BC plans per critical process** | Clause 8.4 | Structured BC plans covering: activation criteria, response structure, roles, communication procedures, resource requirements, and step-by-step recovery procedures | High — core deliverable of the standard |
| **BCMS internal audit programme** | Clause 9.2 | Scheduled audit plan with defined frequency, auditor independence requirements, audit procedures, and audit report templates | High — required for certification |
| **Management review records** | Clause 9.3 | Documented management review meetings with prescribed inputs (audit results, BC performance, exercise outcomes) and outputs (improvement decisions, resource needs) | Medium — required for certification |
| **Exercise and testing programme** | Clause 8.5 | Structured exercise schedule with defined types (tabletop, simulation, full-scale), frequency, scenarios, success criteria, and post-exercise review process | Medium — currently ad-hoc DR drills only |
| **Supply chain continuity strategies** | Clause 8.3 | Specific strategies for continuity of supply chain and critical suppliers beyond vendor risk assessment — alternative supplier identification, stockpiling, diversification | Medium — partially covered by Vendor Risk Management Policy |
| **BC communication plan** | Clause 8.4.3, 7.4 | Formal communication plan for BC incidents: who to notify, when, by what means; includes employees, customers, regulators, media, and other stakeholders | Medium — incident response covers internal escalation only |
| **Competence and awareness records** | Clause 7.2, 7.3 | Evidence that personnel involved in BC are competent and aware of the BC policy, their roles during disruption, and the implications of not conforming | Low — framework-level concern |
| **BC policy statement** | Clause 5.2 | Standalone BC policy document (current coverage is distributed across availability and incident response policies) | Low — content exists but not in ISO-prescribed standalone format |
